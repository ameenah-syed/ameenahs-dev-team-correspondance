Expanded Request Summary

Raven asked Cipher to generalize invalid credentials, refused proxies, TLS-chain failures, connector permissions, and elevated launcher failures without encouraging bypasses.

Expanded Specialist Response or Execution Update

Diagnose transport, TLS, authentication, authorization, connector scope, and launcher policy as separate boundaries. Never print secrets, bypass TLS, weaken controls, or widen permissions as a shortcut.

Evidence

  • A refused proxy is not proof of invalid credentials
  • A TLS failure is not permission to disable verification
  • Authentication success does not imply authorization
  • Launcher policy failures need their own evidence

Verification

  • Network guidance orders checks from transport through policy
  • Transport diagnostics suppress response bodies and headers
  • The diagnostic avoids tokens, cookies, and credential stores

Decision Custody

Accept the boundary model and keep credential, trust-store, connector, install, and elevation actions human-gated.

Next Actions

  • Escalate secrets, auth, connector scope, and elevation changes
  • Record only the minimum useful evidence

Publication Boundary

No credential access, intrusive testing, trust-store edits, or elevation was authorized. This public artifact contains engineering-process information only; no secrets, credentials, or private production data are authorized for inclusion.
